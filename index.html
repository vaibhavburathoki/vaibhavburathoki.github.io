About Me

Scholar, Educator,
Researcher

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at IIT Delhi, where I lead the Computational Intelligence & Health Informatics (CIHI) Lab. My work sits at the frontier of machine learning theory and its applications to real-world scientific problems.

Before joining IIT Delhi, I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, working on statistical methods for single-cell genomics. I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology under Professor Jane Doe, where my dissertation received the George M. Sprowls Award.

My research has been funded by the DST, DBT, Wellcome Trust, and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. I serve on the programme committees of NeurIPS, ICML, and ICLR, and am an Associate Editor of the Journal of Machine Learning Research.

Research

Areas of Inquiry

01

Causal Machine Learning

Developing principled frameworks that move beyond correlation to understand cause-and-effect relationships in high-dimensional observational data, with applications to treatment effect estimation and policy evaluation.

02

Biomedical Informatics

Applying machine learning to accelerate biomedical discovery — including single-cell RNA sequencing analysis, drug-drug interaction prediction, and clinical outcome modelling from electronic health records.

03

Algorithmic Fairness

Investigating how machine learning systems encode and perpetuate societal biases, and designing algorithms with provable fairness guarantees across diverse demographic groups.

04

Graph Representation Learning

Advancing graph neural network architectures for molecular property prediction, knowledge graph completion, and social network analysis with formal expressivity guarantees.

05

Bayesian Deep Learning

Integrating probabilistic reasoning with deep learning to produce calibrated uncertainty estimates essential for high-stakes decision making in medicine and science.

06

Federated & Privacy-Preserving ML

Building learning systems that operate on distributed, sensitive data without compromising individual privacy — critical for healthcare and financial applications.

Research Vision

Questions I Want
to Pursue in a PhD

These are the problems that keep me up at night — areas where I see genuine gaps, and where I believe rigorous research could make a meaningful difference.

Idea 01
Culturally-Adaptive Language Models

How can LLMs be fine-tuned or prompted to reason in ways that are culturally coherent — not just linguistically translated — across the Global South? Can we build evaluation frameworks that go beyond word-level accuracy?

Why now → 80% of the world's languages remain severely underrepresented in current models.
Idea 02
Human–AI Collaborative Reasoning

Can we design AI interfaces that actively strengthen human reasoning rather than replace it? I want to study the cognitive science of AI-assisted decision making and build systems with "productive friction" that keep humans genuinely in the loop.

Why this matters → As AI automates cognition, understanding the effect on human agency is urgent.
Idea 03
Auditing Implicit Values in Generative AI

RLHF instils values into models — but whose values? I want to develop interpretability-based auditing tools that surface the implicit moral and political assumptions baked into the RLHF process of large-scale models.

Why it's open → No systematic methodology exists for values auditing at scale.
